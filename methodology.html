1. What is an alcohol unit?

Mindrink uses a simple, internationally recognised definition:

1 unit = 10 grams of pure alcohol

This system is used by WHO, Australia, Ireland, and many major research studies.
It allows all drinks ‚Äî beer, wine, spirits, cocktails ‚Äî to be compared on the same scale.

To estimate units:

Units = (Volume in ml √ó ABV% √ó 0.789) √∑ 10

Mindrink calculates this automatically, but here are quick examples:

  • Beer (568 ml, 5%) ‚Üí ~2.2 units
  • Wine (175 ml, 12%) ‚Üí ~1.7 units
  • Spirits (25 ml, 40%) ‚Üí ~0.8 units

Different countries count units differently.
For a deeper explanation, see our How to Count Alcohol Units guide.

4. Official Public-Health Sources We Use

Mindrink uses three national guidelines and two international binge definitions.
All values in this section are used as published, with only unit conversion (to 10 g) applied.

1. Canada ‚Äî CCSA 2023 (Strict level)

Source: Canadian Centre on Substance Use and Addiction, 2023.

  • Identifies 0‚Äì2 drinks per week as "low risk."
  • Standard drink = 13.45 g ‚Üí converted to 10 g units.
  • Used for: Mindrink's Strict weekly limit (5 units).

2. Australia ‚Äî NHMRC 2020 (Balanced level)

Source: NHMRC 2020.

  • ‚â§ 4 drinks/day
  • Standard drink = 10 g ethanol
  • Used for: Mindrink's Balanced guideline (daily and weekly caps).

5. Values Mindrink Derives (to unify the model)

Guidelines differ in structure, so Mindrink derives some values to create a coherent, easy-to-understand system.

1. Daily limits for Strict & Relaxed tiers

Canada and Ireland give weekly numbers only.
For consistency, Mindrink applies the Australian daily limit:

Daily limit (blue) = 4 units

2. Weekly high-risk threshold (red)

No guideline defines a universal "high-risk week."

Mindrink sets:

Weekly red threshold = 20 units (‚âà 200 g)

Why:
Large studies (e.g., Lancet 2018) show mortality and cardiovascular risk increasing significantly beyond ~200 g/week.

3. Unisex Strict & Balanced tiers

Canada and Australia are sex-neutral.
Mindrink preserves this because:

  • It reflects the original guidelines
  • Sex differences already appear in binge thresholds

4. Colour zones

Blue, Purple, and Red are Mindrink's interpretive layers, built to help users understand exposure at a glance.

They reflect:

  • Within limit
  • Above limit
  • High-risk pattern

5. Monthly limits

No guideline defines monthly alcohol limits.
Mindrink derives:

Monthly = Weekly √ó 4

This supports long-term trend analysis.

6. Limits vs. Real Life

Mindrink provides guidance, not rules.

Life doesn't always fit into perfect weeks.
Some days you may drink more, some weeks less.
That's normal.

What matters is awareness, not perfection:

  • Noticing patterns
  • Understanding how alcohol affects you over time
  • Making choices aligned with your own goals
  • Reducing harm, not feeling guilt

Mindrink's philosophy:
Guidance, not guilt.
Awareness, not judgment.

You decide what works for your life.
