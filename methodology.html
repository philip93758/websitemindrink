How Mindrink thinks about alcohol limits

We believe in transparency. This page explains our methodology—how we calculate units, where guidance levels come from, and why we focus on awareness rather than strict rules. It's a simplified approach, and we're honest about that.

What is an alcohol unit?

An alcohol unit is a standard way to measure the amount of pure alcohol in a drink. In Mindrink, we calculate it simply:

Volume (ml) × ABV (%) ÷ 1000 = Units

This gives you a consistent measure across all types of drinks, whether it's beer, wine, or spirits.

Common examples:

  • Beer: A standard pint (568ml) at 5% ABV ≈ 2.8 units
  • Wine: A medium glass (175ml) at 12% ABV ≈ 2.1 units
  • Spirits: A single shot (25ml) at 40% ABV ≈ 1 unit

For more details, see our article on how to count alcohol units.

How we derive guidance levels

The daily and weekly guidance levels in Mindrink are based on public health recommendations from various health organizations. These are general guidelines, not personal prescriptions.

We present them as:

  • Daily limits: A suggested maximum for a single day
  • Weekly limits: A suggested maximum spread across the week

These limits come from simplified versions of official guidelines. They're meant to help you understand general recommendations, not to create strict rules. Everyone's body, metabolism, and situation are different.

Limits vs real life

Mindrink shows you guidance levels, but we don't believe in guilt or strict adherence. The limits are tools for awareness, not rules to follow blindly.

Some days you might have more. Some weeks you might have less. That's life. What matters is awareness—knowing what you're drinking, how it affects you, and making choices that align with your goals.

Our philosophy: guidance, not guilt. Use these numbers to build awareness, not to judge yourself.
