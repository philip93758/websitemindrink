How to count alcohol units (without going crazy)

What is an alcohol unit?

An alcohol unit is a standard way to measure the amount of pure alcohol in a drink. It helps you compare different types of drinks on an equal basis—whether it's a pint of beer, a glass of wine, or a shot of spirits.

The formula is simple:

Volume (ml) × ABV (%) ÷ 1000 = Units

Examples with beer, wine, and spirits

  • Beer: A standard pint (568ml) at 5% ABV = 2.8 units
  • Wine: A medium glass (175ml) at 12% ABV = 2.1 units
  • Spirits: A single shot (25ml) at 40% ABV = 1 unit
  • Cocktail: A margarita (150ml) at 15% ABV = 2.25 units

The key is knowing the volume and the ABV (alcohol by volume percentage). Most bottles and cans list the ABV, and standard serving sizes are usually consistent.

Why Mindrink shows units instead of only "drinks"

A "drink" can mean different things to different people. A large glass of wine isn't the same as a small one. A strong beer isn't the same as a light one. Units give you a consistent measure.

By tracking units, you get a clearer picture of your actual alcohol consumption. This awareness helps you make more informed choices, whether you're trying to stay within guidelines or just understand your patterns better.
