How to track alcohol consumption (without turning it into a rulebook)

Introduces alcohol tracking as a way to gain clarity rather than impose rules or restrictions.

Many people avoid tracking alcohol because it feels restrictive. They associate tracking with rules, limits, or judgment.

Tracking as observation, not control

But tracking doesn't have to prescribe behavior. At its simplest, it's a way to externalize memory. Instead of guessing how often or how much you drink, you can see it.

What tracking actually reveals

For some, tracking confirms things are fine. For others, it reveals patterns they hadn't noticed. In both cases, the value lies in clarity, not correction.
