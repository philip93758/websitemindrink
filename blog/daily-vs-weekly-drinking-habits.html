Daily vs weekly drinking: what actually matters when understanding habits

Breaks down the difference between daily frequency and weekly totals when reflecting on alcohol use.

Daily drinking and weekly drinking totals often get conflated, but they describe different things.

Frequency vs volume

Daily drinking speaks to habit. Weekly totals speak to volume. Neither alone explains impact or meaning.

Why daily habits feel different

Someone drinking small amounts most days may experience alcohol very differently from someone who drinks larger amounts less often. Understanding habits requires looking at both frequency and distribution.

How weekly framing changes perception

Weekly framing helps reduce moral thinking. It shifts attention from "good days" and "bad days" to overall rhythm.
